How to Accurately Calculate Your 3D Printing Costs

May 1, 2023 by Vishal Patra

Whether you're running a 3D printing business or just want to track your hobby expenses, calculating the true cost of 3D printing is essential. While many people only consider the cost of filament, there are several other factors that contribute to the total cost of a print.

The Main Components of 3D Printing Costs

1. Material Costs

This is the most obvious cost - the filament used in your print. To calculate this accurately:

  • Find the weight of your print (in grams)
  • Determine the cost of your filament per gram (filament price √∑ 1000)
  • Multiply the weight by the cost per gram

Formula: Print weight (g) √ó Filament cost ($/g)

2. Electricity Costs

Your 3D printer consumes electricity, which adds to the total cost:

  • Determine your printer's power consumption (in watts)
  • Calculate the energy used in kilowatt-hours (print time √ó wattage √∑ 1000)
  • Multiply by your electricity rate ($/kWh)

Formula: Print time (hours) √ó Printer wattage (W) √∑ 1000 √ó Electricity rate ($/kWh)

3. Labor Costs

If you're selling prints or running a business, factor in your time:

  • Estimate the time spent on setting up the print
  • Include post-processing time
  • Assign an hourly rate for your labor

Formula: Total time spent (hours) √ó Hourly rate ($)

4. Printer Depreciation

Your printer has a limited lifespan and will eventually need replacement:

  • Estimate your printer's lifespan (in printing hours)
  • Divide the printer cost by its lifespan
  • Multiply by the hours of the print

Formula: (Printer cost √∑ Estimated lifespan hours) √ó Print time (hours)

5. Failed Print Costs

Not every print succeeds. Account for failures:

  • Estimate your failure rate (percentage)
  • Add this percentage to your total cost

Formula: Base cost √ó (1 + Failure rate)
